Transfer-Based Scaffolded Immersion

A structured, research-informed framework for accelerated, measurable language acquisition.

Designed to move learners from foundation to conversation through positive transfer, frequency-driven vocabulary, task-based application, and scaffolded immersion.

What Is the TSI Method™

TSI Method™ is a language learning framework, not a single app or textbook. It combines established second-language acquisition research with practical implementation.

It is designed to be measurable, scalable, and adaptable across languages and learner backgrounds.

The TSI Method™ focuses on how adults actually acquire language: by leveraging what they already know, prioritizing what is most useful, applying language in meaningful contexts, and entering immersion with preparation rather than guesswork.

The TSI™ Learning Architecture

Positive Transfer

Leveraging cognates, derivatives, and structural similarities between languages to reduce cognitive load and accelerate early comprehension.

Frequency-Based Lexicon

Prioritizing the most common words and verb structures that account for the majority of real-world usage.

Functional Grammar

Teaching grammar as usable patterns, not abstract rules, enabling early sentence formation.

Task-Based Application

Applying language through media and structured real-world tasks that reinforce comprehension and cultural context.

Scaffolded Immersion

Transitioning into immersive environments after learners have built a functional linguistic foundation.

How Progress Is Measured

TSI Method™ does not rely on vague "fluency" claims. It tracks concrete metrics aligned with CEFR standards:

Vocabulary Benchmarks

  • A2: ~1,000 active words
  • B1: ~2,000 active words
  • B2: ~2,500–3,000 active words

Skill Development

  • Active vocabulary size
  • Passive vocabulary recognition
  • Time-on-task (hours of exposure)

FSI Time Expectations

1100 hrs
